About OUTSINC

OUTSINC (Outreach Someone In Need of Change) was created by people who have lived it – sleeping outside, navigating shelters, facing addiction, fighting for housing, and trying to make sense of systems that often feel cold and confusing.

We know what it's like to tell your story over and over and still not get the help you need. We also know what a difference it makes when even one person shows up, listens without judgement, and walks through the hard parts with you instead of talking down at you.

OUTSINC exists to be that kind of support.

We are a community-based, peer-informed support and navigation service. Our team works alongside people who are homeless, under-housed, using substances, dealing with mental health challenges, or simply feeling stuck. We help you figure out what you want to work on next, what options are available, and how to get there — step by step, at your pace.

We are not here to "fix" you. We are here to stand with you.

We partner with local services, outreach teams, shelters, hospitals, and community groups to make it easier to move between systems without falling through the cracks. We also work with businesses, neighbours, and community members who want to respond to visible homelessness, drug use, and crisis with care instead of shame.

OUTSINC is built on harm reduction, human rights, and the belief that everyone deserves dignity, safety, and a place to belong — regardless of income, substance use, housing status, or what their life looks like right now.

Our Mission

To provide compassionate, person-centred support and navigation for people impacted by homelessness, substance use, mental health challenges, poverty, and systemic barriers — meeting people where they are, helping them define their own goals, and walking with them as they build safer, more stable, and more hopeful lives.

Our Vision

We envision a community where:

  • No one is left outside without options
  • People can access help without shame, judgement, or impossible rules
  • Lived and living experience is treated as expertise
  • Systems work together instead of pushing people around
  • Housing, health, safety, and belonging are treated as basic human rights

Our Core Values

Person-Centred – You are the expert on your own life. We follow your priorities, your pace, and your definition of "better."
Lived Experience at the Centre – Many of our team members and peers have been homeless, used substances, or navigated the same systems. That experience shapes everything we do.
Harm Reduction – We support people who use substances in staying as safe as possible. We don't require abstinence to offer help, and we don't give up on people because of use.
Accessibility – We remove barriers wherever we can: flexible contact options, multiple ways to connect, plain language, mobile-friendly tools, and support for people who don't have ID, phones, or stable addresses.
Self-Determination – You choose your goals. You can say yes, no, or "not right now." We help you make informed choices, not choices for you.
Acceptance & Non-Judgement – We work with people in crisis, intoxicated, angry, tired, hopeful, numb, or all of the above. You don't need to be "polite" to deserve help.
Collaboration – We don't replace existing services; we connect them. We work with shelters, clinics, outreach teams, community groups, and local businesses to coordinate support.
Accountability & Transparency – We are clear about what we can and cannot do, what information we collect, and how it's used. If we make a mistake, we own it and work to repair it.
Advocacy & Systems Change – Individual support matters, but so does changing the conditions that keep people stuck. We share what we see with decision-makers, grounded in real stories and data.

Who We Support

OUTSINC supports people who are:

Sleeping outside, in tents, vehicles, or unsafe places
Staying in shelters, motels, or constantly moving between couches
Struggling with substance use, including opioids, stimulants, alcohol, or multiple substances
Living with mental health challenges, trauma, or ongoing stress
Dealing with violence, exploitation, or unsafe relationships
Coping with grief, loss, or big life changes
Facing barriers with ID, income, court, probation, child welfare, or school/work
Just feeling overwhelmed, stuck, or unsure where to turn next

You do not need a diagnosis, a referral, or "the right words" to ask for help. If something in your life isn't working and you want support to change it — even a little — you're welcome here.

What We Offer

Navigation & One-to-One Support

🧭

System Navigation & Advocacy

We help you understand what services exist (housing, detox, treatment, mental health, legal, income, ID, family support, outreach, etc.) and how to access them.

  • Help fill out forms and applications
  • Go with you to appointments when possible
  • Make calls or send emails with you or on your behalf
  • Advocate when you're not being heard or taken seriously
Get Started
🎯

Goal Setting & Planning

We sit down with you — on the street, at a drop-in, over the phone, or online — and ask: What would you like to see change in your life over the next days, weeks, or months?

Together we build a realistic plan with small steps, backup plans, and check-ins that match your actual life, not a textbook.

Get Started
📅

Ongoing Check-Ins

Support can be one-time or ongoing. We can check in regularly to see what's working, what fell apart, and what needs to shift.

Life isn't linear; our support isn't either.

Get Started

Harm Reduction Support

💊

Supplies & Safer Use Support

We connect people with harm reduction supplies (e.g. safer use equipment, naloxone, safer smoking/injection supplies) through outreach, partner agencies, and local programs.

We focus on:

  • Reducing overdose risk
  • Reducing infections and injuries
  • Keeping people as safe and informed as possible
Get Started
🚨

Overdose Prevention & Response

We offer:

  • Education on overdose risks and warning signs
  • Naloxone training and distribution through partners
  • Support after an overdose, including connection to services if you're ready
Get Started
💬

Non-judgemental Conversations about Use

We talk openly about substance use without shaming or lecturing. If you want to reduce, change, or stop using, we can help you explore options. If you're not ready or not interested, we still care about your safety and wellbeing.

Get Started

Housing, Income, and Practical Supports

🏠

Housing Navigation

  • Help with housing applications, waitlists, and housing searches where possible
  • Support with landlord communication and housing rights information
  • Safety planning for people staying outside, couch-surfing, or in unsafe places
Get Started
💳

Income & ID Support

  • Help applying for or stabilizing income (social assistance, disability benefits, etc.)
  • Support with replacing or obtaining ID (birth certificate, health card, etc.)
  • Assistance reading letters and understanding deadlines so nothing gets missed
Get Started
🔧

Daily-Life Problem-Solving

Not everything fits in a program box. We can help with things like:

  • Preparing for court dates or meetings with workers
  • Organizing paperwork
  • Breaking down overwhelming tasks into smaller, doable pieces
Get Started

Mental Health, Trauma, and Wellness Support

We are not a crisis line or a replacement for professional therapy. We are:

🧠

Support and Connection

  • A listening ear when you need to talk
  • A safe space to process what's going on
  • A bridge to other services that may be helpful

We can help you:

  • Explore coping strategies that might actually fit your life
  • Talk about trauma, grief, and loss at your pace
  • Connect to mental health supports (counselling, groups, crisis teams, peer programs) if and when you're ready
  • Create personal safety and crisis plans

Support for Businesses & Community Members

Visible homelessness and substance use can be hard on everyone. Businesses, neighbours, and community members often care deeply but feel stuck and unsure how to respond.

OUTSINC offers:

Consultation & Support for Businesses

  • Guidance on responding to unsheltered people with dignity and safety
  • Help creating compassionate policies for washrooms, loitering, and complaints
  • De-escalation tips and harm reduction information
  • A place to ask questions without being labelled "heartless" or "uncaring"
Contact Us

Reporting & Follow-Up

A way to report concerns about tents, encampments, welfare checks, or discarded harm reduction supplies, where those reports are handled with care for both safety and human dignity.

Quick, confidential reporting for:

  • Harm reduction supplies (needles, etc.)
  • Welfare checks
  • Abandoned belongings
  • Encampments or tents
  • Safety concerns

Partnership & System Collaboration

Outreach teams and peer programs
