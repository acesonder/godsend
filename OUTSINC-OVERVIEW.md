# OUTSINC – Outreach Someone In Need of Change

## Overview

**Name:** OUTSINC – Outreach Someone In Need of Change

**Purpose:** A unified, trauma-informed platform to:
- Support people facing homelessness, substance use, mental health, poverty, and related crises
- Help outreach/shelter staff and service providers coordinate care
- Give businesses/public a safe way to report concerns and get guidance
- Turn real, on-the-ground experience into data and stories that shift systems

## Core Principles

Core principles baked into the design:
- **Person-centred, harm reduction, trauma-informed**
- **Lived experience drives design and feedback**
- **"Tell your story once"** – no more re-traumatizing info dump every new worker
- **Tech supports human relationships, not the other way around**

## Roles & Access Levels

Everything in the system orbits around clearly defined roles.

### Primary Roles

1. **Client / Peer**
   - People accessing help and services
   - **Access:** their own profile, survey answers, appointments, messages, some resources, their consents

2. **Outreach / Case Worker / Shelter Staff**
   - People doing direct support: outreach, intake, case management, harm reduction, follow-up
   - **Access:** client profiles (assigned or role-appropriate), notes, assessments, tasks, reports, limited analytics

3. **Service Provider / Partner Agency**
   - External services (Fourcast, shelters, clinics, legal, etc.) with whom you share or receive referrals
   - **Access:** clients they're connected to, referrals, consents, limited info needed to support

4. **Admin / System Coordinator**
   - People running OUTSINC, managing users, settings, modules, analytics
   - **Access:** configuration, user management, full reporting, logs, feature flags

5. **Public / Business / Community Member (Anonymous or Limited Account)**
   - People reporting concerns (encampments, needles, welfare checks) or seeking info
   - **Access:** public resources, reporting tools, FAQs, contact forms
